Why Simplify?

  • OnTrac + P2P are both regional consolidators — having two is redundant
  • USPS is operationally undesired — not great to work with
  • Volume commitments constrain routing — OnTrac 279K/year, USPS 140K/year must be met regardless of cost efficiency
  • FedEx $5M undiscounted threshold — dropping below triggers a $500K contractual penalty on top of losing the 16% earned discount

The Goal

Reduce to fewer providers, cut costs, eliminate volume commitments, and maintain the FedEx earned discount.

How We Tested

15 Scenarios: Simulation → Implementation

We modeled every feasible provider combination across 15 scenarios. Our methodology follows a two-step process:

Step 1: Simulation

Pick the cheapest option per shipment — the theoretical lower bound. Not configurable in SPE, but tells us whether a direction is worth pursuing.

Step 2: Implementation

Translate promising simulations into static weight-based rules configurable in SPE. Expect ~3% cost increase ($0.1–0.2M) from simplification.

Key constraint: FedEx undiscounted spend must stay above $5M to avoid a $500K penalty and maintain the 16% earned discount. Any scenario that drops FedEx below this threshold faces an automatic $500K penalty on top of the rate increase from losing the discount.

FedEx Earned Discount

Maintaining the $5M Undiscounted Threshold

FedEx applies a 16% earned discount on Home Delivery when undiscounted annual spend exceeds $4.5M. Below $5M, a $500K penalty applies. S15 is designed to stay above both.

How Undiscounted Spend is Calculated
